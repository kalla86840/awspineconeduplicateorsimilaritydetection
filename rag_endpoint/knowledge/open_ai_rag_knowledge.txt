# Washer And Dryer Setup Manual
This manual explains general steps for connecting a household washer and dryer. Always read the manufacturer manual for the exact appliance model before installation. If local code requires a licensed installer, or if you are unsure about electrical, gas, plumbing, or venting work, stop and contact a qualified professional.

# Safety Before Installation
Turn off electrical power before connecting or moving appliances. Turn off the water supply before connecting washer hoses. If installing a gas dryer, turn off the gas supply before connecting the gas line. Do not use damaged power cords, crushed hoses, kinked vents, or leaking gas connectors. Keep children and pets away from the work area.

# Washer Water Connections
Place the washer close enough to the water hookups so the hoses are not stretched. Connect the hot water hose to the hot water inlet and the cold water hose to the cold water inlet. Hand-tighten the hose connections, then tighten slightly with pliers if the appliance manual allows it. Do not overtighten. Turn the water supply on slowly and check both ends of each hose for leaks.

# Washer Drain Connection
Insert the washer drain hose into the standpipe or laundry sink according to the washer manual. The hose should be secured so it cannot jump out during draining. Do not seal the drain hose airtight into the standpipe because the washer needs an air gap for proper drainage.

# Leveling The Washer
Set the washer in its final position and adjust the leveling feet until the washer is stable. A washer that rocks can vibrate, walk across the floor, or become noisy during spin cycles. After leveling, tighten the lock nuts on the feet if the appliance includes them.

# Electric Dryer Connection
For an electric dryer, confirm the outlet matches the dryer cord and the circuit meets the appliance rating. Connect the dryer power cord according to the dryer manual and local electrical code. Many dryers require either a three-prong or four-prong cord depending on the home outlet. If the plug, outlet, or wiring does not match, contact a qualified electrician.

# Gas Dryer Connection
For a gas dryer, use only an approved flexible gas connector. Apply pipe-joint compound only where the dryer manual allows it and never on flare fittings unless instructed. After connecting, turn on the gas and check for leaks using an approved leak-detection solution. If you smell gas or see bubbles, turn off the gas, ventilate the area, and contact a qualified professional.

# Dryer Vent Connection
Connect the dryer exhaust outlet to a rigid or semi-rigid metal vent. Avoid plastic or foil vents because they can restrict airflow and increase fire risk. Keep the vent as short and straight as possible. Secure the vent connection with clamps, not screws that protrude into the duct. Confirm the outside vent flap opens freely.

# Final Startup Check
Push the washer and dryer into place without crushing hoses, cords, gas connectors, or vents. Run a short washer cycle and check for water leaks and proper draining. Run the dryer on an air or low-heat cycle and confirm air exits the outside vent. Recheck that both appliances are level, stable, and operating without unusual noise or smell.

# When To Call A Professional
Call a professional if you need a new electrical outlet, a changed dryer cord, a gas line connection, a new shutoff valve, a leaking supply line repair, or a new dryer vent route. Stop using the appliance immediately if there is a burning smell, gas smell, water leak, sparking, repeated breaker trips, or abnormal vibration.
